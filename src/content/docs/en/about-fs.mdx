Formula Student is a global student engineering competition in which university teams design, build, and validate a single-seat formula-style race car.

## Competition Overview

Formula Student started in 1981 in the United States and has grown into one of the largest engineering competitions for university teams worldwide.

<Aside type="tip">
Formula Student is not only about speed. Teams are judged on engineering quality, cost, business presentation, reliability, and execution.
</Aside>

## Categories

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Combustion" icon="rocket">Internal-combustion race cars focused on mechanical design and powertrain tuning.</Card>
  <Card title="Electric" icon="rocket">Battery-electric race cars focused on motor control and energy systems.</Card>
  <Card title="Driverless" icon="laptop">Autonomous race cars operating without a human driver.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Event Types

- Static events: design, cost, and business
- Dynamic events: acceleration, skidpad, autocross, endurance, and efficiency
- Driverless events include autonomous dynamic challenges and full track driving

## Why Teams Participate

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Engineering Practice" icon="open-book">Apply classroom knowledge to a real vehicle program.</Card>
  <Card title="Teamwork" icon="approve-check">Work with students from different disciplines on one shared product.</Card>
  <Card title="Career Growth" icon="rocket">Build experience that is directly relevant to automotive and robotics work.</Card>
  <Card title="Global Perspective" icon="star">Learn from how teams across the world solve similar engineering problems.</Card>
</CardGrid>
