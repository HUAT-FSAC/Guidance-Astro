### Main unit installation requirements

<Steps>
1. Mount it rigidly to the car body or a rigid bracket
2. Avoid locations exposed to direct water spray
3. Keep it away from strong vibration sources such as the engine; keep more than 30 cm away when possible
4. Avoid areas with large temperature swings
5. Leave at least 15 cm of clearance around the main cable connector
6. Leave more than 1 cm of clearance from nearby structure and enough room for installation tools
7. Keep it away from EMC interference sources and at least 20 cm away from large-current harnesses above 3 A
8. Do not remove or move it after calibration unless you plan to recalibrate
9. Keep the mounting surface parallel to the vehicle horizontal plane
10. Keep the device axis close to vertical alignment requirements
11. Ground the localization-module housing properly
</Steps>

### Antenna installation requirements

**Placement**

- keep the two antennas more than 1 m apart
- keep antenna pitch angle under 20 degrees
- keep them at least 30 cm away from strong electromagnetic interference sources
- mount them on a stable and reasonably flat plane

**Cable routing**

- keep feeder cables away from other harnesses and noise sources
- avoid tightly bundling the antenna cables with unrelated wiring

**Common layouts**

1. slave antenna in front, main antenna in back, aligned roughly north-south
2. main antenna on the left, slave antenna on the right, aligned east-west
3. slave antenna about 30 degrees north-east of the main antenna
4. slave antenna about 40 degrees north-west of the main antenna

## Calibration

### Wiring

<Image src={imgWiring} alt="INS wiring" />

<Aside type="note">According to vendor support, the USB port is only for receiving data and cannot be used for host-side configuration.</Aside>

### Host-side serial configuration

<Image src={imgSerial} alt="Host serial configuration" />

<Steps>
1. refresh the serial-port list
2. select the USB serial port connected to the INS
3. open the port with the configured baud rate; if you change it here, update the matching launch configuration in the ROS driver as well
</Steps>

### RTK service configuration

Configure the differential-account credentials from the Qianxun controller app.

<Aside type="tip" title="Qianxun account">
Qianxun usually sponsors this event and provides a one-year service account to teams each season. Ask the team lead for the current season's account details.
</Aside>

**Method 1: Connection page**

<Image src={imgNtrip} alt="Connection-page RTK configuration" />

Configure the fields and click `connect ntrip`.

**Method 2: Settings page**

<Image src={imgRtk} alt="Settings-page RTK configuration" />

Open the `5711` settings, fill in the RTK parameters, and apply them.

When the RTK indicator below turns blue, the configuration succeeded.

### Device calibration

<Steps>
1. **Restore factory settings**

   In the settings page, click restore factory settings, save and reboot, wait about 10 seconds, then read the factory parameters and confirm the displayed offsets are all zero.

2. **Enter installation offsets**

   Park the vehicle level outdoors and observe the view page. Then write the corresponding offsets in the settings page:
   - use the negative of the roll and pitch values from the INS protocol in the parameter settings
   - set antenna-installation roll to zero and write pitch and heading from the GNSS protocol directly
   - measure the x, y, z distance from the INS center to the rear-axle center, with vehicle front as positive x, vehicle right as positive y, and downward as positive z

3. **Run calibration**

   Enter calibration mode. When the status changes to automatic calibration in progress, begin logging in the tools page and drive a pattern that includes left turns, right turns, and straight segments. When the status changes to calibration succeeded, stop logging and then save and reboot from the settings page.

</Steps>

## Common fault analysis

### Status LEDs

<Image src={imgLed} alt="INS status LEDs" />

**Power LED abnormal**

1. check the power wiring
2. confirm supply voltage is within 9 to 32 VDC
3. confirm current draw is normal, around 0.5 A

**Work-status LED abnormal**

1. check the GNSS LED state
2. open the host software and verify the PPS light blinks normally
3. check whether GNSS-protocol pitch exceeds 20 degrees

**GNSS LED abnormal**

- off: check sky visibility and antenna cabling
- slow blink: check differential configuration, 4G connectivity, and whether the differential account expired
- fast blink: usually means no fixed RTK solution yet

**4G LED abnormal**

- blinking: check differential-account configuration and expiration
- off: check whether the SIM is installed and still active

## ROS driver

Update the launch file according to the configured baud rate and actual device connection on the onboard computer:

```xml
<!-- connection type: serial port = 0, UDP = 1 -->
<param name="ConnectionType" type="int" value="1" />

<!-- serial device path; default: /dev/ttyUSB0 -->
<param name="UART_Port" type="string" value="/dev/ttyUSB0" />

<!-- baud rate; default: 115200 -->
<param name="UART_Baudrate" type="int" value="230400" />
```

Before launching, grant the device permission:

```bash
sudo chmod 777 /dev/ttyUSB0
```
