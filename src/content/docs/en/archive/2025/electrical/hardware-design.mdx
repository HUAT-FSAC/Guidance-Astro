## Power management

### Power topology

```
12V Battery ─→ DC-DC ─→ 5V ─→ LDO ─→ 3.3V
```

### Key parts

| Conversion | Load | Suggested Part |
|------|------|----------|
| 12V to 5V | 3A | LM2596 / TPS54331 |
| 5V to 3.3V | 500mA | AMS1117-3.3 |

## Signal acquisition

### Analog signals

- **Pedal position** - 0-5V linear potentiometer
- **Steering angle** - angular sensor
- **Temperature** - NTC thermistor / PT100

### Digital signals

- **Wheel speed** - Hall-sensor pulse counting
- **Switch inputs** - opto-isolated digital input

---

## PCB rules

### Layout principles

1. place the power entry near the connector
2. keep analog signals away from digital-noise sources
3. keep high-speed traces short and direct
4. widen high-current traces or use copper pours

### Design software

- **Altium Designer** - common professional choice
- **KiCad** - open-source and free
- **LCSC EDA / JLCEDA** - domestic option with easy manufacturing flow

---

## EMC design

### Anti-interference measures

1. **Filter capacitors** - for example 100μF + 0.1μF at the power input
2. **TVS diodes** - transient suppression
3. **Ferrite beads** - high-frequency noise isolation
4. **Shielding can** - protect sensitive circuits

<Aside type="caution">
The electromagnetic environment of a race car is harsh. EMC design is not optional.
</Aside>
