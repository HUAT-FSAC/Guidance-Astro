## Design principles

The vehicle wiring harness is the electrical circulatory system of the car. The design should satisfy:

1. **Safety** - proper high-voltage and low-voltage isolation
2. **Reliability** - water resistance, vibration resistance, and thermal durability
3. **Maintainability** - modular design for inspection and service

---

## Harness topology

### High-voltage system

```
Battery Pack ─→ Main Relay ─→ Motor Controller ─→ Drive Motor
                  ↓
             Precharge Circuit
```

### Low-voltage system

```
Low-Voltage Battery ─→ PDU ─→ VCU
                         ↓
                  Sensors / Actuators
```

---

## Wire-size selection

| Use Case | Current | Recommended Cross-Section |
|------|------|----------|
| HV main loop | 200A | 35mm² |
| Motor controller | 100A | 16mm² |
| LV power | 30A | 4mm² |
| Signal wire | `&lt;1A` | 0.5mm² |

<Aside type="tip">
When selecting wire gauge, account for temperature rise and keep roughly 30% design margin.
</Aside>

## Connector selection

### High-voltage connectors

- **Amphenol** - HVP series
- **TE Connectivity** - HVSL series
- key requirements: IP67 sealing and interlock support

### Low-voltage connectors

- **Deutsch** - DTM series
- **Molex** - Micro-Fit series

---

## Routing rules

<Steps>
1. route HV and LV harnesses separately
2. keep signal wiring away from large-current harnesses
3. avoid sharp bends; minimum bend radius should exceed five times the wire diameter
4. support spacing should be no more than 200 mm
5. add sleeves or grommets when routing through metal holes
</Steps>
