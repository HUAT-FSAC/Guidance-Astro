## System overview

The braking system is the final safety barrier of the race car and must remain reliable under every operating condition.

---

## Brake-force distribution

### Ideal brake-balance target

The front-rear brake balance is estimated from the vehicle parameters:

| Parameter | Value |
|------|------|
| Static axle-load split | 48:52 |
| Load transfer under braking | 15% |
| Ideal brake-force split | 60:40 |

### Pedal ratio

- pedal ratio: 5:1
- pedal travel: 100 mm
- max pedal force: 400 N

---

## Brake-component selection

### Brake calipers

The design uses **Wilwood** four-piston calipers:
- piston diameter: front 32 mm / rear 28 mm
- material: 6061-T6 aluminum alloy

### Brake discs

| Parameter | Front Disc | Rear Disc |
|------|----------|----------|
| Diameter | 240 mm | 220 mm |
| Thickness | 5 mm | 4 mm |
| Material | ductile cast iron | ductile cast iron |

<Aside type="tip">
Lightweight alloy discs can reduce mass, but you need to reassess heat rejection and wear carefully.
</Aside>

---

## Thermal management

### Temperature monitoring

- brake-disc operating temperature: 300-500°C
- required brake-fluid boiling point: above 260°C for DOT 4

### Cooling design

1. **Venting features** - slots or drilled patterns in the disc
2. **Cooling ducts** - direct airflow to the brakes
3. **Heat shields** - protect wheel bearings and nearby components

---

## Driverless braking redundancy

<Aside type="danger">
A driverless race car must include a braking redundancy system.
</Aside>

### EBS

**Emergency Brake System**:

1. **Primary braking** - motor regenerative braking plus hydraulic braking
2. **Redundant braking** - an independent pneumatic or electric brake actuator
3. **Fault detection** - monitoring through brake-pressure sensing

### Trigger conditions

- communication loss
- sensor failure
- manual trigger from remote control or mushroom switch
