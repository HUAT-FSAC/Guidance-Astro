## Suspension overview

The suspension connects the wheels to the chassis and directly affects handling performance and vehicle stability.

### Suspension concept

The car uses a **double-wishbone independent suspension**. Main advantages:

- large range for geometry adjustment
- strong tire contact behavior
- well suited to high-speed cornering

### Key parameters

| Parameter | Front | Rear |
|------|------|------|
| Track width | 1200 mm | 1180 mm |
| Kingpin inclination | 8° | - |
| Caster | 4° | - |
| Camber | -2° | -1.5° |
| Toe | 0.5° | 0° |

## Steering system

### Steering-ratio design

Steering-ratio selection is a balance between:
- steering sensitivity
- driver effort
- high-speed stability

<Aside type="tip">
Use previous vehicle data and driver feedback as the starting point for tuning.
</Aside>

### Ackermann geometry

The design targets **100% Ackermann** geometry so that the inner and outer wheels follow a more appropriate steering-angle relationship at low speed.

## Damper selection

### Candidate brands

- **Ohlins TTX** - high-performance adjustable dampers
- **Bilstein** - a strong cost-performance option
- **Domestic alternatives** - worth considering when cost pressure is high

### Tuning points

1. compression damping - controls roll response in corners
2. rebound damping - affects how quickly tire grip recovers
3. preload - adjusts ride attitude and setup window
