## System overview

The drivetrain transfers motor power to the driven wheels and is one of the key systems behind the car's acceleration performance.

---

## Motor selection

### Drive-motor parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|------|------|
| Rated power | 35kW |
| Peak power | 80kW |
| Peak torque | 230Nm |
| Max speed | 6000rpm |

<Aside type="note">
Driverless race cars place a high premium on response speed, so motor-controller behavior matters as much as the motor itself.
</Aside>

---

## Reduction stage

### Gearbox design

The current concept uses a **single-stage planetary reducer**:
- reduction ratio: 1:4.5
- transmission efficiency: 96%
- compact and lightweight layout

### Design considerations

1. **Gear-strength analysis** - verify contact and bending stress
2. **Lubrication strategy** - splash lubrication versus forced lubrication
3. **Thermal design** - manage heat under sustained load

---

## Half-shaft design

### Material choice

- **4340 alloy steel** - high strength and fatigue resistance
- **Constant-velocity joints** - maintain smooth torque transfer while steering

### Failure analysis

Common failure modes:
- torsional-fatigue fracture
- spline wear
- CV-joint seizure

---

## Differential

The design uses a **limited-slip differential (LSD)**:
- improves corner-exit acceleration
- reduces single-wheel spin in low-grip conditions
