## Platform comparison

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Fssim">
    ### Fssim

    **Overview**: The first simulation platform the author encountered, originally developed by AMZ Racing.

    **Tech stack**:
    - ROS1 on Ubuntu 18.04
    - Gazebo and RViz for simulation and visualization

    **GitHub**: [AMZ-Racing/fssim](https://github.com/AMZ-Racing/fssim)

    **Usage notes**:
    - switch track environments by modifying the relevant SDF files
    - launch the required nodes to start the simulation

    <Image src={imgSimInfo} alt="Modify track information" />

    The figure below shows a high-speed-tracking simulation node setup:

    <Image src={imgSimNode} alt="Launch simulation nodes" />

    **Typical use cases**: skidpad, acceleration, and high-speed tracking style event simulation

  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="FSDS">
    ### FSDS (Formula Student Driverless Simulator)

    **Overview**: A platform the team learned about through exchanges with other teams.

    **Tech stack**:
    - Unreal Engine 4 + AirSim
    - very high visual fidelity

    **GitHub**: [FS-Driverless/Formula-Student-Driverless-Simulator](https://github.com/FS-Driverless/Formula-Student-Driverless-Simulator)

    <Aside type="caution">
    Hardware demand is high, especially on the GPU side.
    </Aside>

    **Recommendation**: currently one of the most practical and reliable options if the hardware budget allows it

  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="CARLA">
    ### CARLA

    **Overview**: More commonly used for general autonomous-driving scenarios such as urban traffic.

    **GitHub**: [carla-simulator/carla](https://github.com/carla-simulator/carla)

    <Aside type="note">
    For Formula Student style tracking work, CARLA is often too large and too complex, and its hardware requirements are also high. Use it only if you have a clear reason.
    </Aside>

  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="f1tenth">
    ### f1tenth_gym_ros

    **Overview**: A lightweight simulation environment mainly designed for F1TENTH miniature race cars.

    **GitHub**: [f1tenth/f1tenth_gym_ros](https://github.com/f1tenth/f1tenth_gym_ros)

    **Install guide**: [f1tenth Simulator on Ubuntu20 with ROS2 Foxy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h-0HjJzuo6Q)

    <Aside type="caution">
    The match to our competition format is not especially strong. Some data such as PID parameters may be useful only as a reference and may not transfer safely to the real car.
    </Aside>

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Personal view on simulation

<Aside type="tip">
    In Formula Student Driverless, simulation may contribute a bit during review and presentation, but for everyday engineering it is only a partial reference for real-car testing.
</Aside>

Based on the most recent review cycle, judges seem to care less about simulation as an isolated concept and more about **whole-vehicle testing**.

**Core view**:

- simulation is mainly there to give you rough indicators before real-car testing
- it cannot replace the full physical test process
- it cannot eliminate the gap between simulation and the real vehicle

**Simple example**:

> In simulation, the mechanical structure is fixed. On the real car, the mechanical behavior is never perfectly predictable and may deviate a lot from your expectation. In that sense, the Fuzhou University line from 2024 is still persuasive: "We skipped simulation and tuned directly on the real car."

**Recommended resource**: [FSAE-TJU Formula Student simulation platform](https://www.yuque.com/baiyunqianzaijin/vggzyn/ogmpsb29e8gbtf5p)

## Review summary

The following questions were raised during this year's review.

### Simulation and testing

| No. | Question |
| :--: | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 | How is overall testing planned? |
| 2 | Is there a plan for the full-vehicle design and test process? |
| 3 | How are hardware-selection decisions made? |
| 4 | Is testing done step by step from small to large, or directly on the real car? |
| 5 | How far along is the vehicle overall, what remains weak, and what unresolved issues still exist? |
| 6 | Is there supporting documentation, including code-management history and issue-resolution records? |
| 7 | In testing, do you use controlled-variable methods or mixed validation methods, and did you verify the relevant techniques? |

### Driverless-system questions

| No. | Question |
| :--: | ------------------------------------------------ |
| 8 | At the highest level, what factors limit the team's progress? |
| 9 | What exactly counts as the driverless system, and how is the whole architecture arranged? |
| 10 | Who owns the overall planning, and were records kept? |
| 11 | Has time synchronization been implemented? |
| 12 | Are there safety redundancy measures, and how are they implemented? |
